About Zermatt

Nestled at the foot of the iconic Matterhorn in the Swiss Alps, Zermatt is a car-free alpine village known for its world-class skiing, hiking, and mountaineering. With breathtaking mountain scenery, charming village atmosphere, and excellent dining options, Zermatt offers something for everyone in our family.

We've created personalized "Top 5" lists for each family member based on their specific interests. Scroll down to find activities tailored just for you!

Jason's Top 5

Hiking trails, scenic viewpoints, and historical sites

1. Five Lakes Trail (5-Seenweg)

This spectacular hiking trail takes you past five alpine lakes, each offering a different perspective of the Matterhorn. The trail is moderately challenging but rewards hikers with some of the most iconic views in Switzerland, including perfect reflections of the Matterhorn in Stellisee on calm days.

Starting Point: Blauherd station (accessible via Sunnegga-Rothorn funicular and gondola)

Duration: 2.5-3 hours for the complete circuit

Tip: Start early in the morning for the best chance of seeing the Matterhorn reflected in Stellisee.

2. Gornergrat Railway & Hiking

Take Europe's highest open-air cogwheel railway to the 3,089m Gornergrat ridge for breathtaking panoramic views of 29 peaks over 4,000m, including the Matterhorn and Monte Rosa. The historic railway has been operating since 1898 and is an engineering marvel.

Journey Time: 33 minutes from Zermatt to Gornergrat

Hiking Options: Several trails of varying difficulty levels

Tip: Consider buying a one-way ticket up and hiking part of the way down to experience the alpine terrain up close.

Donna's Top 5

Chocolate experiences, wine tastings, specialty local goods, and shopping districts

1. Bahnhofstrasse Shopping

Zermatt's main street, Bahnhofstrasse, offers a delightful car-free shopping experience with a mix of luxury boutiques, Swiss watch shops, chocolate stores, and outdoor gear retailers. The picturesque street is lined with charming buildings and has the Matterhorn visible at its southern end.

Location: Central Zermatt, running north-south through the village

Best Time: Weekday mornings tend to be less crowded

Tip: Many shops close for a lunch break between 12-2pm. The southern end of the street offers the best Matterhorn views for shopping photos.

2. Chocolate Tasting at Läderach

Indulge in premium Swiss chocolate at Läderach's Zermatt boutique, where you can sample their famous FrischSchoggi (fresh chocolate) slabs in various flavors. Läderach is known for using high-quality ingredients and traditional Swiss chocolate-making techniques.

Location: Bahnhofstrasse, central Zermatt

Specialty: FrischSchoggi (fresh chocolate slabs), Matterhorn-shaped chocolates

Tip: Ask about their seasonal specialties, which often incorporate local alpine flavors like Swiss honey or mountain herbs.

Caleb's Top 5

Adventure activities, local cuisine specialties, and unique dining experiences

1. Matterhorn Glacier Paradise Ski Experience

Experience the unique thrill of summer skiing on Europe's highest and largest summer ski area at the Theodul Glacier. With over 21 km of perfectly groomed pistes at altitudes between 2,900m and 3,883m, you can enjoy skiing while the rest of Europe is sweltering in summer heat.

Location: Theodul Glacier, accessed via Matterhorn Glacier Paradise

Season: Year-round, including June

Tip: Ski in the morning when snow conditions are best. The contrast of skiing while wearing sunscreen and light layers is a unique experience.

2. Paragliding Over the Matterhorn

Soar like an eagle on a tandem paragliding flight over Zermatt for an unforgettable aerial perspective of the Matterhorn and surrounding Alps. Experienced pilots will handle the technical aspects while you enjoy the sensation of free flight and spectacular views.

Departure Points: Rothorn or Schwarzsee (weather dependent)

Duration: 15-30 minutes flight time

Tip: Book early in your stay to allow for rescheduling if weather conditions are unfavorable.

Ryan's Top 5

Indoor activities, tech attractions, and scenic vistas requiring minimal hiking

1. Matterhorn Glacier Paradise Experience

Visit Europe's highest cable car station and viewing platform at 3,883m with minimal physical effort. The state-of-the-art "Matterhorn Glacier Ride" cable car features a glass floor and takes you to the summit of Klein Matterhorn for breathtaking 360° panoramic views of the Swiss, Italian, and French Alps.

Location: Klein Matterhorn, accessed via cable car from Zermatt

Altitude: 3,883m (12,739 feet)

Tip: Visit early in the morning when visibility is usually best. The Glacier Palace maintains a temperature of -3°C year-round, so bring warm clothing.

2. Zermatlantis Matterhorn Museum

Explore the fascinating underground museum that tells the story of Zermatt's development from a farming village to a world-famous mountain resort. The museum is built around an archaeological site showing the remains of a reconstructed 19th-century alpine village.

Location: Kirchplatz, central Zermatt

Duration: 1-2 hours

Tip: Don't miss the holographic display showing the timeline of Matterhorn ascents and the virtual reality station that simulates standing on the Matterhorn summit.
